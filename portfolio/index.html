Lava Bug


Feb 2024

Name: Molten Scarab (aka Lava Bug) Species: Invertebrate. Insecta. Scarabaeidae

The aliens have been named Molten Scarbs, and nicknamed Lava Bugs. They are similar to scarabs or beetles of Earth, however their exoskeleton mimics molten rock in order to blend in with their natural environment. The small bugs are about the size of a walnut but feel like you are holding a baseball. They seem to be non-hostile but the public is given little information as NASA and the government work together to research them for any hostile tendencies and diseases.

Trapped Creativity


May 11, 2024

My inspiration for this work is the overwhelming creative block that I have been building up. I feel that I have been doing art so much more for school than for myself. Even in these photos you can see the computer has Canvas open. The majority of my creativity has been dedicated to my school projects that I have little to spare in my free time. Ignoring past projects and drawing that I have left incomplete for years. The ghost drawing shown in these photos is one that I have drawn since I was a freshman in high school. Something I constantly draw to represent myself, almost like an artist persona. I used to make comics with it all the time but now I have been putting all my notebooks in storage to make room for school projects. In the first photos you can see I am storing them next to my trash can. So in this piece I wanted to show my childhood creativity trying to be free while I am dedicated to school in the background. But in the end instead of locking that side of myself up I decide to make space for it and myself. To make time for myself and my own personal projects.

Manufactured Femininity


Dec 6, 2023

Corsets in the past made it so women can modify themselves into this manufactured mold of what femininity was at the time. They enlarge the butt and breast by making their waist so thin you can wrap your fingers around it. Today these standards pushed onto female bodies are slowly eroding. People are breaking out of these molds and embracing who they are. So in my piece, Manufactured Femininity, I wanted to show this erosion of past molds. I was heavily inspired by old corset ad photos showing women with super thin waists. Specifically, I found the Warner’s “Rust-Proof” corset . I thought it was ironic that I am making a rusty corset based off of their “Rust-Proof” one. So I used it as a guide while I made my piece.

Chachayotes

Apr 19, 2024

I made Chachayotes out of ceramic and I used steel wire and leather to attach the pieces together. Chachayotes are traditionally made out of leather with ayoyote seed shells sewed on to them. They are worn by Aztec dancers who will wear these on their legs, arms, and have rattles with similar seed shells. This means a lot to me since my mom is an Aztec dancer. I am Native American but I didn’t grow up with a lot of my culture. So the few things I do have I cherish. Especially since there is an extreme amount of Native American cultural erasure that goes back decades.
